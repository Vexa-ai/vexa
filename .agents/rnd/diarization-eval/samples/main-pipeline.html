What main actually does

Teams already runs its own speaker diarization and shows it in live captions. Main just reads that: the browser tags every audio frame with the caption's current speaker name (__vexaTeamsAudioData(name, …)), and a caption speaker change triggers flushSpeaker(previous) — that's the entire "speaker change" mechanism. Audio is routed by speakerId = 'teams-' + name. There is no VAD, no segmentation model, no embeddings, no clustering. Whisper only re-transcribes each speaker's buffer for the published text.

So the whole diarization problem in main is outsourced to Teams

Who = caption name. When (boundary) = caption speaker change. The bot never has to figure out speakers itself.

Why the pack (#394) replaces this

Caption-driven diarization breaks when: live captions are off / not permitted, lag or mis-attribute, or aren't available for the language. The pack swaps the green block for pyannote+wespeaker so diarization is self-contained and doesn't depend on Teams captions — but that's exactly what introduced the segmentation+clustering work we've been doing.

Implication for our direction

The cut-at-switch idea is the bridge: keep main's clean "switch → flush → fresh buffer → Whisper" shape, but drive the switch signal from our own segmentation instead of Teams captions — so it works without captions, without depending on the weak clustering for routing.
