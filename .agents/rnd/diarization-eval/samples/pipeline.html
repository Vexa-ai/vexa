① Segmentation — works (placement good)

pyannote finds where the speaker changes within ~0.16s. Cost: it re-runs on a sliding 10s window every 250ms (~40× redundant inference = the CPU spikes), and over-emits duplicate boundaries that need consolidating.

② Cut triggers — mostly scaffolding

The real cut is a pyannote boundary. The change-point detector and the 3s timeout are band-aids: one covers pyannote's missed switches (using the weak embedding), the other bounds latency + missed-boundary damage. Both are symptoms of "streaming + imperfect segmentation."

③④ Embed + cluster — THE WEAK LINK

wespeaker (trained on clean VoxCeleb celebrity audio) can't separate our speakers on mixed meeting audio — embeddings barely move at real switches. The online cosine/EMA clustering then mislabels and scatters. Because downstream routes the buffer by speakerId, a wrong label sends audio to the wrong Whisper buffer → contamination. This is what every fix targets.

⑤⑥⑦ Buffer + Whisper + publish — fixed, out of scope

Per-speaker buffering, LocalAgreement confirmation, Whisper, and publishing stay exactly as they are in vexa-bot. Our job is only to hand this stage correct (speakerId, audio) cuts.

Deepgram (our ground truth) uses the SAME shape

segment → embed → cluster — but with a 100k-speaker conversational embedder, run in batch. The architecture isn't wrong; the embedder + online penalty are. Alternatives: stronger embedder (③), or Sortformer end-to-end (replaces ③④ with a learned per-frame speaker track).
