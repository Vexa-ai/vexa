Read it in three lanes

① Browser (blue): startTeamsRecording taps the meeting media stream with a 4096-sample ScriptProcessor and fires __vexaTeamsAudioData per frame. Teams captions are scraped separately into __vexaTeamsCaptionData.
② Node — capture & diarize: handleTeamsAudioData buffers each frame in teamsPendingFrames (stamped Date.now()) and feeds it to teamsDiarizer.process(). The diarizer does VAD → accumulate → pyannote cut → wespeaker embed → cluster (weak) → onCommit.
③ Node — route & transcribe: onCommit names the segment by raw cluster id, drains the matching frames out of teamsPendingFrames, dual-routes overlap, and calls speakerManager.feedAudio. From there it's the unchanged per-speaker buffer → Whisper → publish path.

Two things specific to Teams worth noting

• Captions are vestigial for naming. The cutover (#394) made the diarizer's cluster id authoritative; teamsAttributor still records captions but no longer drives the published speaker label (grey path).
• Two clocks. Audio frames carry wall-clock Date.now(); the diarizer's commit times are session-relative samples. onCommit bridges them by draining a sample-count's worth of FIFO frames rather than matching timestamps — a known sharp edge.

Where the cut-at-switch prototype changes this

It would replace EMB→CLU→onCommit-by-clusterId with: cut on the segmentation switch → new buffer → feedAudio with a provisional/decoupled label. Lanes ① and ③ stay; only the red middle changes.
